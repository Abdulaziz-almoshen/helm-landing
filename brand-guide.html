01 — Brand Essence

The idea behind Helm

Every professional captures information all day — voice memos, meeting notes, photos, scattered thoughts. The tragedy is that most of it dies where it was born. Notes become graveyards. Ideas rot in messaging apps. Action items vanish between apps.

Helm exists to solve one problem: the gap between capturing a thought and acting on it. Our AI doesn't just store — it thinks, classifies, prioritizes, and hands you a specific next action for everything.

Mission

Turn scattered knowledge into structured action — automatically, in seconds.

Vision

A world where no professional idea is lost, forgotten, or left without a next step.

Position

The AI knowledge engine for professionals who think in Arabic, English, or both.

Brand Promise

"Your brain captures.
Helm does the rest."

This is the one sentence that captures everything. The user's job is to think, decide, and capture. Helm's job is everything after: structure, priority, action, memory.

02 — Logo System

The Helm Mark

The Helm mark represents our core promise: scattered inputs converging into structured action. Three dots (chaos) flow into a bold checkmark (clarity). The gradient connects our warm, human brand to the precision of AI.

Primary — Dark background

Light background variant

On gradient — white mark only

Logo Anatomy

The Three Dots

Represent scattered inputs — voice memos, meeting notes, photos, ideas. Deliberately uneven in size and opacity to convey organic, human messiness.

The Checkmark

Represents structured action — the output. Bold, confident, glowing. The orange-to-amber gradient conveys warmth and energy. The glow filter adds depth and modernity.

Clear Space & Minimum Size

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the checkmark stroke around all sides. Minimum size: 24px for digital, 10mm for print.

05 — Liquid Glass Design Language

Depth without weight

Inspired by Apple's Liquid Glass, Helm's UI language uses translucent layers, frosted blur, and subtle light refraction to create depth without heaviness. Every surface feels like it floats above the content behind it.

Translucent depth
Background blur + inner light + subtle border = glass that breathes.

Rules

✓ Do

Use glass for nav bars, floating cards, badges, overlays, and CTAs. Glass works best when there's visible content behind it. The 135deg light refraction gradient must always go top-left to bottom-right.

✕ Don't

Don't use glass on static content with no depth context. Don't stack more than 2 glass layers. Don't use glass blur below 20px — it looks muddy. Never omit the inset top highlight — it's the key to the glass illusion.

07 — Value Proposition

Three pillars. One promise.

Every feature, every screen, every word in Helm serves one of these three pillars. If something doesn't serve a pillar, it doesn't belong.

Capture anything

Voice, text, photos, PDFs, scans — mixed freely in one thread. No templates, no formatting. Offline-capable. The lowest friction capture experience possible.

Key message

"Dump it all. Helm sorts it out."

AI thinks for you

Six-layer pipeline in ~6 seconds: extract facts, classify urgency (Eisenhower Matrix), plan actions, research context, map people, build memory. Not "smart search" — structured reasoning.

Key message

"It doesn't just record. It reasons."

Never drop the ball

Every input produces a specific next action — not "follow up" but "Send Ahmed the 500K decision by Thursday." Tasks auto-prioritized. People tracked. Nothing falls through.

Key message

"A next step for everything."

Competitive Frame

What we replace

Apple Notes + Notion + WhatsApp voice notes + manual task management + "I'll remember that" (you won't).

What we are NOT

Not a note-taking app (we structure, not store). Not a project management tool (we feed them). Not a chatbot (we reason, not chat).

08 — Localization & Cultural Identity

Arabic is not a feature.
It's our identity.

Helm was born for the Saudi professional market. Arabic isn't bolted on — it's the foundation. RTL layout, Hijri calendar awareness, ZATCA regulatory context, and natural Arabic output are defaults, not options.

Language Detection

The app auto-detects Arabic, English, or mixed input. Output always matches the input language. An Arabic voice memo produces Arabic tasks, minutes, and action hints. Never translates unless asked.

Default: Arabic

The landing page defaults to Arabic for Saudi visitors (browser language detection). The app UI follows system language. All marketing materials are bilingual.

RTL Implementation

Full RTL support via dir="rtl" at the document level. All layouts use flexbox/grid — no floats. Border-left accents become border-right. Chevrons flip. Text alignment reverses. The Noto Kufi Arabic typeface is loaded alongside Urbanist.

Cultural Context

Hijri calendar in date references. Saudi Riyal (SAR) with official symbol in pricing. ZATCA compliance in business examples. Local names (Ahmed, Munira) in demos. This is not localization — it's authenticity.

i18n Architecture

The landing page uses a custom i18n engine (i18n.js) with 289 English keys and 260 Arabic keys. Translation is attribute-based: data-i18n for text, data-i18n-html for rich content, data-i18n-attr for attributes.

Detection Priority

1. URL param (?lang=ar)
2. localStorage (helm_lang)
3. Browser language
4. Default: Arabic

Language Toggle

Always visible in nav. Glass-styled pill button. Switches instantly — no page reload. Persists choice in localStorage.

Content Parity

All marketing copy must exist in both languages before publishing. Arabic is written natively, never machine-translated. Tone and specificity must match.

09 — Experience Principles

Design like Steve.
Ship like Jony.

These principles govern every design decision in Helm — from pixel spacing to pipeline architecture. When in doubt, return here.

01

Capture friction = 0

The distance between "I have a thought" and "it's captured" must be as close to zero as possible. No templates. No categories. No formatting. Open thread, talk, done.

02

Show the reasoning

AI is a black box elsewhere. In Helm, every classification shows its reasoning. "Why Do Now? Because Ahmed's contract has a Thursday deadline with 500K implications." Transparency builds trust.

03

Action, not information

The output is never "here's your summary." It's "here's your next action, prioritized, with a deadline and an owner." Every screen ends with a verb.

04

Human in the loop

AI proposes, human confirms. The workspace agent never writes data without approval. This isn't a limitation — it's a feature. "Two-phase: AI proposes, you confirm."

05

Every note makes the next one smarter

The Knowledge Engine learns goals, people, patterns, facts. Capture 10 notes about Ahmed and the 11th already knows his role, your relationship, and the context. Compounding intelligence.

06

Depth through glass

Every layer of the UI communicates its depth through translucency. Cards float. Backgrounds breathe. Light refracts. The interface feels alive — not flat, not skeuomorphic, but liquid.
